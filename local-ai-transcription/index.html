What "local AI" actually means here

Most "AI-powered" editing tools send data to a remote model hosted by the vendor. CaptionLab downloads the AI model itself to your machine and runs inference locally — the same underlying technique used by on-device AI features in modern phones and laptops, applied to video transcription.

Local AI vs cloud AI for transcription

Cloud AI transcription typically means faster iteration on the vendor's side (easier to update the model) at the cost of your data leaving your machine and often per-minute billing. Local AI trades a one-time setup and hardware dependency for privacy and no ongoing cost — for editors handling sensitive footage, that trade is usually worth it.

Frequently asked questions

Which AI model does CaptionLab run locally?
OpenAI's Whisper large-v2 model, via faster-whisper on Intel Mac/Windows or mlx-whisper on Apple Silicon.
Do I need a powerful computer for local AI transcription?
It runs on any Mac with macOS 11+ or Windows 10/11. Apple Silicon or an NVIDIA GPU speeds things up, but CPU-only also works, just slower.
Is local AI transcription as accurate as cloud AI?
Yes — it's the same Whisper model architecture either way; running it locally doesn't change the underlying accuracy.
