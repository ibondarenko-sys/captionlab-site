What to look for in a subtitle plugin

🌍

Language coverage

Adobe's built-in tool covers ~16 languages. If you edit in Ukrainian, Polish, Turkish, Arabic, or dozens of others, you need something broader.

🔒

Where your footage goes

Cloud-based tools upload your video to a server. For client or NDA work, that's often a non-starter — offline processing matters.

💰

Pricing model

Subscription vs one-time purchase makes a real difference if captioning is only part of your workflow, not your whole job.

🧩

Native integration

Does it live inside Premiere Pro, or does it mean exporting, uploading, waiting, then re-importing?

🎯

Accuracy

Word-level timestamps and clean segmentation save real editing time versus a rough, hard-to-fix transcript.

🔑

No API keys required

Some "plugins" are just thin wrappers around a paid API — worth knowing before you're billed per minute.
