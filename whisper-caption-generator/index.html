Captions vs a raw transcript

A transcript is one long block of text; captions are that same text broken into short, readable, correctly-timed chunks for display on screen. CaptionLab generates the caption-ready version directly — segmented SRT, not a wall of text you'd have to chop up yourself.

Does it generate a plain transcript too, or only captions?
The output is a segmented SRT file — usable directly as captions, and easy to read as a transcript too since it's just timestamped text.
Can I control caption line length or reading speed?

Does it support burned-in captions, or only SRT?
