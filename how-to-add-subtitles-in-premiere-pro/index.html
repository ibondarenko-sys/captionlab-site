How to add subtitles in Premiere Pro

Three ways to caption a video in Premiere Pro, from fully manual to fully automatic — including what to do if your project isn't in one of Adobe's ~16 supported languages.

Option 1 — Adobe's built-in Speech to Text

1

Open the Text panel

Window → Text, then go to the Transcript tab.

2

Transcribe your sequence

Click "Transcribe Sequence" — Adobe processes the audio and generates a transcript.

3

Create captions

From the transcript, click "Create Captions" to generate a caption track on your timeline.

Works well for English, Spanish, French, German, and Adobe's other supported languages. If your spoken language isn't on that list, this won't work — skip to Option 3.

Option 2 — Manual captioning

Window → Text → New Caption Track, then type out each caption by hand, setting in/out points manually. Works for any language, but is slow — typically 3 to 5 hours per hour of footage.

Option 3 — CaptionLab (for any of 99 languages)

1

Install CaptionLab

A Premiere Pro extension — drag-and-drop install, no Terminal required.

2

Select a clip or sequence

Pick the spoken language or leave it on auto-detect.

3

Generate Subtitles

CaptionLab transcribes locally using OpenAI's Whisper model and produces a word-level accurate SRT.

4

Drop it on your timeline

The SRT imports straight into your Premiere project as an editable caption track.

Covers all 99 languages Whisper supports — including Ukrainian, Polish, Turkish, Arabic, Indonesian, and dozens more that Adobe's built-in tool doesn't offer. Runs fully offline, one-time $39.
