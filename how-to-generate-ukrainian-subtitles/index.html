A note on accuracy for Ukrainian specifically

Ukrainian has some characters and grammar quirks (і, ї, є, ґ; verb conjugation patterns) that trip up speech-recognition models trained mostly on English data. Whisper, the model CaptionLab uses, was trained on a genuinely multilingual dataset that includes substantial Ukrainian audio, which is why it handles it meaningfully better than English-first tools that bolt on other languages as an afterthought.

Frequently asked questions

Is there a free way to generate Ukrainian subtitles?
Free options exist (running Whisper yourself via command line, for instance) but require technical setup. CaptionLab packages the same underlying model into a one-time $39 Premiere Pro plugin with no setup required.
Will it handle surzhyk or regional dialects?
Whisper's accuracy on non-standard dialects and heavy code-switching varies and is generally less reliable than for standard Ukrainian — worth reviewing the output more carefully in those cases.
Can the subtitles be translated to English afterward?
Yes — CaptionLab can translate directly to English as part of generation, or you can transcribe in Ukrainian first and translate separately.
